**Why are people applying for a PhD straight out of undergrad?**

In physics and related fields, most PhD programs don’t require a master’s degree for admission. Additionally, while most PhD programs will pay you, many master’s programs require you to pay to attend. Therefore, many STEM students will go straight into a PhD program following undergrad. That said, many PhD programs will allow you to get a master’s degree en route. In some cases, if you realize you don’t want to finish your PhD then you can “master out,” meaning you defend a master’s thesis and leave the PhD program.

However, a master’s degree can be helpful for some students who feel they need to strengthen their application to be a competitive PhD or industry applicant. There are even some UNC students who have hustled and found funded master’s degrees (eg. Math at Wake Forest), although these are very rare. Either way, it’s important to talk to a research or academic advisor who can help you figure out which path is right for you.
