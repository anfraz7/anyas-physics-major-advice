[Here](https://registrar.unc.edu/order-a-transcript/) is the UNC transcript page. 

Many schools will ask for an unofficial transcript that contains your name, the name of the school, and an explanation of the grading scale at UNC. Unfortunately, the UNC version of the unofficial transcript doesn’t contain the school name or grading scale! Therefore, I downloaded an official transcript from Parchment (about $10) and submitted that to all my applications.


### about parchment

Parchment says to allow for 2-3 business days for the electronic transcript to send, but I have requested several official transcripts while at UNC and they have always delivered within an hour or two (including once on a Sunday and once on Thanksgiving break). So if you find yourself in a pinch day of, you’re probably okay! That said—--order your transcripts ahead of time to be safe.

YOUR TRANSCRIPT WILL BE ENCRYPTED! When you download your official transcript from Parchment, it will be password protected so that only you can access it and they will not give you the password. If you submit an encrypted transcript to a grad school, they won’t be able to open it. To un-encrypt your transcript, use [this](https://smallpdf.com/unlock-pdf#r=unlock) website. Easy! It may seem sketchy, but a quick google search will reveal hundreds of graduate school applicants on Reddit across the years doing the same thing. 

### choose your best transcript

Grad school applications are due right around the end of fall semester. If your fall semester grades look like they will help your GPA, wait until they come in and submit your applications then. If they will hurt your GPA, submit your applications before the semester ends.


### non-UNC transcripts

Most schools will ask for all college-level transcripts. For transfer students, that’s pretty straightforward. This technically also includes study abroad transcripts and high school dual enrollment transcripts. I don’t *think* any school will actually dock you if you don’t send these (I forgot to send my dual enrollment transcript to two schools that accepted me) but it’s better to be safe than sorry. It could be important if one of these transcripts contains a class that counts towards your degree.
