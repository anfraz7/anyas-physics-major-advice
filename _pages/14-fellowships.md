### external fellowships

I applied for the NSF GRFP and DOE CSGF this year and wasn’t awarded either so I won’t pretend to be an expert. There are lots of great resources and example applications out there for both (like [this](https://www.alexhunterlang.com/nsf-fellowship) and [this](https://github.com/felker/doe_csgf_applications)!). It’s super helpful to have a research advisor or trusted grad student to help you with applications like this that require a proposal. The writing center is not going to be great for the GRFP proposal, but I did find it helpful for the CSGF because the application specifically requests a proposal that is understandable to anyone outside of your field.

There are a few discipline-specific fellowships out there. In my case I applied to the DOE CSGF which is for computational sciences. Do your research to figure out what fellowships apply to you. Here’s a [random reddit page I found](https://www.reddit.com/r/gradadmissions/comments/y6mw7z/list_of_graduate_fellowshipscholarship/).


### internal fellowships

I received the University Fellowship at Penn State, where I did an REU last summer and continued research this past year. It definitely helps to have a connection with a research group at a university, because my understanding is that often professors can recommend students they like for their university’s fellowships. Sometimes you can form a connection by reaching out early in the summer and setting up a meeting with a research group. Ultimately though, I don’t think you can do much to increase your chances of getting an internal fellowship other than just putting together the best overall application you can to each school.
