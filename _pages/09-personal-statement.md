The Personal Statement (PS) is the essay that is also called the Personal History Statement, the Diversity Statement, etc. Not every school asks for it, and if they do I’ve heard that it’s particularly used for fellowship determination. It's your time to talk about (1) mentorship programs you led, science outreach, and student leadership, (2) explain your unique life perspectives that will enrich the grad school program, and/or (3) your chance to turn a negative part of your application (low GPA, etc) into a memorable story of triumphing over hardship (without making excuses). I have found this essay sooo tricky because what???! That's so vague! Also, it requires a fine line between personal reflection _outside_ of your career that reveals more about why you will be an excellent researcher _in_ your career.

To be honest, I don’t think that I truly knocked the PS out of the park. I'm therefore not going to dwell on my thoughts. Instead, [here's](https://www.reddit.com/r/gradadmissions/comments/nl0boo/diversity_statements_101_an_essay_guide_for/) a really great reddit article that I found after the fact which addresses a lot of different fears about the PS very well. The article pushes a more narrative style than many successful PS I have read so it's definitely okay to stray from that a bit, but overall I think it's a splendid article.
