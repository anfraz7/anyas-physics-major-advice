The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is kind of like the SAT of grad school applications. The GRE offers a general exam, which to my understanding is almost directly analogous to the SAT in that it's supposed to test your general well-rounded knowledge or something, as well as specific subject exams including a Physics GRE. I did not take either exam.

Within physics and materials science disciplines, the only ones I can speak to, the GRE is becoming increasingly obsolete. I saw zero (0) schools that required or even recommended the general GRE--in fact, I don't think any of the schools I applied to even allowed me to submit general GRE scores. On the other hand, the physics GRE is still sometimes used for physics PhD admissions. Policies I saw ranged from "you don't have to submit your physics GRE scores but we highly recommend it" to "we will not look at your physics GRE scores do not send them." A very small number of physics PhD programs still require the physics GRE, but it's easy enough to avoid those if you are deadset on not taking it.

### so should I take the physics gre?

Firstly, double check the policies of the schools you are interested in applying to. [Here's](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/19UhYToXOPZkZ3CM469ru3Uwk4584CmzZyAVVwQJJcyc/edit?gid=0#gid=0) a nice spreadsheet of mostly accurate school-by-school GRE policy breakdowns, but it's good to double check all of your school websites just in case. Many people I know specifically created their application lists to avoid having to take the physics GRE, so that's an option. If you're on the fence about taking the physics GRE, ask around to a few physics professors to get their advice on your situation. I chose not to take the physics GRE because only a few of my applications highly recommended it and I had a high GPA, so anything lower than a stellar GRE score would have hurt me. I'm very glad I didn't put the energy into studying for it when I was already so busy leading up the PhD applications. On the other hand a great GRE score could help offset a lower GPA. Again, best just to ask professors if you're on the fence.
